# Student Guide

## Purpose

The student guide explains how learners should use the handbook, track progress, request help, and build portfolio evidence.

## How to Use the Handbook

1. Read the learning roadmap.
2. Choose a learning path.
3. Set a study plan.
4. Complete chapters in order for your path.
5. Complete labs and quizzes.
6. Capture screenshots and validation evidence.
7. Update your progress tracker.
8. Build a capstone project.
9. Prepare to explain your work.

## What Counts as Progress

Progress is based on evidence.

Examples:

- Completed checklist.
- Passing command output.
- Accepted screenshot.
- Quiz score.
- Lab submission.
- Project milestone.
- Reflection answer.

Reading is important, but reading alone is not completion.

## How to Ask for Help

When asking for help, include:

- The chapter or lab.
- The step number.
- What you expected.
- What happened instead.
- Error messages.
- Screenshot or output when safe.
- What you already tried.

## How to Learn from Mistakes

Mistakes are part of technical learning. Record:

- Symptom.
- Likely cause.
- Fix attempted.
- Final solution.
- What you would check earlier next time.

## Portfolio Habits

Keep evidence organized as you study.

Recommended portfolio evidence:

- Project brief.
- Architecture diagram.
- Validation screenshots.
- Quiz completion notes.
- Prompt records.
- Security or quality review.
- Final reflection.

## Study Integrity

You may use AI tools, mentors, and references, but you must understand and validate your final work.

Do not submit work that you cannot explain.
